STS 2024 : 2nd Semi-supervised Teeth Segmentation MICCAI Challenge

MICCAI 2024, MARRAKESH, MOROCCO

October 6-10, 2024

2D Panoramic Xray 👉 CodaBench

For this track, we test the model's teeth instance identification and segmentation capabilities using panoramic Xray images with different ages and medical centers. Among them, the test images may contain cases of caries, and some images may also have missing teeth or incomplete teeth. We aim to test different abilities with different testing images: Location, Segmentation, Classification.

In specific, we provide 2450 high-resolution panoramic Xray images, including 2350 unlabeled training sets, 30 labeled training sets, 20 validation sets, and 100 independent test sets. All images are in JPG format, and the annotation files are in JSON format, following the format of Labelme.

Evaluation

The segmentation inference results are evaluated using Dice Similarity Coefficient (DSC), Normalized Surface Dice (NSD), Intersection over Union (IoU), Panoptic Quality (PQ), Identification Accuracy (IA), Running time (RT) and Area under GPU memory-time curve (GPU-area). All metrics will be used to compute the ranking. We will release the code on how to calculate the 10 performance metrics with the python3 version. The ranking scheme includes the following five steps:

  • Step 1: In order to maintain consistency with our previous challenge and assess the algorithm's performance at the image level, our evaluation framework will first initially compute three metrics, Dice, IoU and NSD, on a per-image basis to derive the algorithm's Dice (image_Dice), IoU (image_IoU) and NSD (image_NSD) scores at image-level.
  • Step 2: Next, given the challenge's focus on instance-level analysis, we also will employ a greedy algorithm to match the corresponding prediction results with the ground truth, allowing for the calculation of Dice (instance_Dice), IoU (instance_IoU), NSD (instance_NSD), IA (instance_IA) and PQ (instance_PQ) scores at the instance level.
  • Step 3: Then, the evaluation will include measurements of running time (RT) and GPU consumption (GPU_area) for each algorithm.
  • Step 4: Take the mean of the ten metrics over the test cases, and rank separately among the teams.
  • Step 5: A final overall rank is given by taking the average of the four ranks. In the case of equal average rankings for two teams, they are considered tied.

Awards

To determine the winners, we will rank the participants based on their final algorithm on the independent test set. We have planned a range of comprehensive awards for this track, which we hope will attract and motivate you to participate.We look forward to your involvement and to seeing the outstanding work that will emerge from this competition.

  • Cash: $500 for the 1st participant.
  • Certificates: The top 10 participants will receive a certificate.
  • Oral presentation: Top-performing participants will be invited to participate in the presentation
  • Paper publication: Teams with outstanding performance can submit papers to our workshops and participate in review papers.
